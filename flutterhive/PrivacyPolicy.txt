## 3. Information We Collect

### 3.1 Information you provide voluntarily

The App is designed as a **UI demo and learning tool**. You are **not required** to provide personal information such as your name, email address, or phone number to browse demo screens.

Some demo screens (for example login, signup, or profile layouts) may display input fields for **design demonstration only**. Unless clearly stated otherwise in a specific live feature, information entered into those demo fields is **not collected or stored by us on our own servers**.

If you contact us by email, we will receive the information you choose to send (such as your email address and message content) so we can respond to you.

### 3.2 Information collected automatically by third-party services

When you use the App, certain third-party services integrated into the App may automatically collect technical and usage information. This may include:

- **Device or other identifiers** (such as advertising ID, Firebase app instance ID, or installation ID)
- **App activity** (such as screens viewed, app interactions, and general usage events)
- **Crash logs and diagnostic data** (such as crash reports and performance metrics)
- **Push notification token** (if notifications are enabled on your device)
- **General device and app information** (such as device model, operating system version, app version, language, and network status)

This data is primarily collected through:

| Service | Provider | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Firebase Analytics | Google | Usage analytics and app improvement |
| Firebase Crashlytics | Google | Crash reporting and stability |
| Firebase Performance | Google | App performance monitoring |
| Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) | Google | Push notifications |
| Firebase Remote Config | Google | Remote app configuration (e.g. app update settings, feature flags) |
| Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) | Google | Displaying advertisements |

We do not sell your personal information.

### 3.3 Information from optional device permissions (demo features only)

Some screens in the App demonstrate how Flutter handles device permissions. These permissions are requested **only when you open the related demo screen** and choose to grant access. Examples include:

| Permission | Used for |
| --- | --- |
| Camera | Camera / gallery demo screens |
| Photos / media / storage | Image, video, audio, and document demo screens |
| Location | Google Maps demo screens |
| Notifications | Notification demo functionality |
| Biometric | Fingerprint / local auth demo screens |

**Important:**

- **Contacts demo** and **SMS demo** screens display **sample (fake) data** for UI demonstration. The App does **not** read your real contacts or SMS messages for upload to our servers.
- Data accessed through optional permissions is generally used **on your device** to show the demo UI and is **not transmitted to our servers**, unless a third-party SDK collects technical data as described in Section 3.2.
